Association between liquor stores and crime rates in New York City.

The alcohol-violence link is well-established in social science research, particularly focusing on the geospatial influence of alcohol outlets on violence. Studies(Gorman et al. 2018; Gruenewald and Remer 2006; Livingston 2008; Pridemore and Grubesic 2013; Roman et al. 2008; White, Gainey, and Triplett 2015; Zhu, Gorman, and Horel, 2004) have consistently shown that areas with higher alcohol outlet density tend to have higher rates of violence and street crimes. This relationship has also been observed in relation to interpersonal violence, domestic violence, suicide mortality, and disadvantaged neighborhood characteristics.

Additional research (Gmel, Holmes, and Studer 2016) has explored the spatial influence of different types of alcohol outlets on crime, indicating that not all types of outlets are equally criminogenic. These studies have primarily compared on-premise (where alcohol is consumed on the premises) and off-premise (where alcohol is sold and distributed off the premises) facilities. They have identified that on-premise facilities, such as bars or restaurants, have different effects compared to off-premise facilities. While there appears to be a correlation, it's important to note that crime rates are influenced by a multitude of factors that may vary by neighborhood. Therefore, this issue warrants a deeper exploration to understand the underlying causes of crime in these areas.

New York City is the largest and most diverse metropolis in the United States, comprising multiple communities spread across five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, the Bronx, Queens, and Staten Island. In the United States, ghettos have historically been associated with racial segregation and discriminatory housing policies such as redlining, which restricted access to mortgage lending and property ownership for people of color. These policies contributed to the creation of urban neighborhoods that were predominantly occupied by low-income African Americans and other minority groups. While the term "ghetto" can be controversial and carries a negative connotation, it is often used in scholarly research and public discourse to refer to areas with high levels of poverty, crime, and social disadvantage. However, it's important to recognize the complexity of these neighborhoods and the various factors that contribute to their formation and persistence.

Police brutality towards the Black community refers to instances where law enforcement officers use excessive force, discriminatory practices, or display biased behavior specifically targeting individuals who are Black or of African descent. It is a deeply concerning issue that has gained significant attention and sparked widespread protests and discussions in recent years.

The history of police brutality against Black individuals in the United States is rooted in systemic racism and a legacy of racial discrimination. Throughout history, Black communities have faced disproportionate levels of violence and aggression from law enforcement agencies. This includes incidents of racial profiling, unjustified use of force, racial bias in arrests and sentencing, and even fatal encounters.

There are several factors that contribute to the issue of police brutality towards the Black community:

  • Racial bias and stereotypes:

    Implicit biases and preconceived notions about Black individuals can lead to discriminatory treatment by some police officers. These biases may influence perceptions of threat or criminality, resulting in excessive force or unjust treatment.

  • Over-policing and profiling:

    Certain neighborhoods with predominantly Black populations are often subjected to heightened police presence and surveillance. This can lead to increased interactions between law enforcement and community members, increasing the likelihood of confrontations and incidents of police brutality.

  • Lack of accountability:

    There have been cases where law enforcement officers involved in incidents of police brutality, particularly against Black individuals, have not faced adequate consequences for their actions. This lack of accountability can contribute to a culture of impunity and perpetuate a cycle of violence.

  • Socioeconomic factors:

    The intersection of socioeconomic disparities and policing can contribute to heightened tensions and interactions with law enforcement. Factors such as poverty, lack of access to quality education, and limited economic opportunities can exacerbate the relationship between police and the communities they serve.

  • Historical context:

    The long history of systemic racism, including slavery, segregation, and discriminatory practices, has created a legacy of distrust between law enforcement and Black communities. This historical context can shape perceptions and interactions, affecting the dynamics between police officers and individuals of African descent.

It is important to note that not all police officers engage in or support acts of brutality. Many officers work diligently to serve and protect their communities. However, the issue of police brutality highlights the urgent need for systemic reforms, including enhanced training on de-escalation techniques, stricter accountability measures, community policing initiatives, and efforts to address underlying racial biases within law enforcement agencies.

Addressing police brutality requires a multifaceted approach that involves community engagement, legislative reforms, and changes in policing practices. It is crucial to promote transparency, accountability, and equity within the criminal justice system to ensure thethe fair and just treatment of all individuals, regardless of their race or ethnicity.

After the aforementioned findings we started looking into the relationship between alcohol use and the growth of crime in the different precincts of the NY city. Retail shops known as liquor stores are dedicated to the sale of alcoholic drinks such beer, wine, and spirits. These shops might also sell food, mixers, and other things complement with alcoholic beverages. Several jurisdictions have stringent laws governing the location, business hours, and who is permitted to buy alcohol at liquor stores. In certain places, liquor stores are run and owned by the government, whereas in other places, they are run and owned by private businesses. Indeed, high levels of alcohol outlet density can contribute to various negative outcomes, including increased alcohol consumption, impaired driving, and violent crime. Pamela J.Trankenstein et al. in a survey have proven that a 10% increase in alcohol outlet access was associated with a 4.2% increase in violent crime exposure. Off-premise outlets such as these liquor stores were found to have a greater association with violent crime than on-premise outlets such as bars or pubs. Other results show that opening a new alcohol outlet increases both violent and property crimes, especially in low socioeconomic status neighborhoods. Also both surveys conclude that violent and property crimes as well as drug offenses are mostly reported in such regions with a high density of liquor stores. Overall the relationship between alcohol outlets and criminality in ghettos is complex. While there is no direct causal link, certain factors contribute to the association. Concentration of disadvantages, accessibility of alcohol, social disorganization, violence and disorder, and targeting vulnerable populations are key factors. The presence of alcohol outlets in ghettos can contribute to increased alcohol consumption and availability, potentially leading to higher rates of criminal behavior. However, it is important to consider contextual factors and individual characteristics. Addressing these issues requires community development, economic revitalization, improved education, access to social services, and targeted interventions for substance abuse. Implementing regulations on alcohol outlet density and location, along with responsible sales practices, can help mitigate negative impacts.
